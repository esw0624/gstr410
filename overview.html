The Hilltop Youth is a radical Jewish nationalist movement based primarily in the West Bank, emerging in the early 2000s as an informal faction within the broader Israeli settler movement. The group’s ideology blends elements of religious Zionism with neo-Kahanist thought, drawing heavily on the teachings of Rabbi Meir Kahane, whose writings advocate Jewish territorial maximalism and the removal of Palestinians from the land (Magid, 2021). Members of the Hilltop Youth frequently reject the authority of the Israeli state, asserting that divine law obligates them to establish Jewish presence across the biblical land of Israel, even through illegal settlement outposts and confrontational actions (Garcia-Navarro, 2012; Tzfadia, 2017).

Across the West Bank, individuals associated with the Hilltop Youth have participated in a range of violent and retaliatory actions often referred to as “price-tag” attacks, which are acts meant to intimidate Palestinians and pressure the Israeli government. These incidents include arson, vandalism of Palestinian mosques and schools, destruction of olive groves, property damage, and physical assaults (Byman & Sachs, 2012; Satherley, 2013). One of the most notorious cases linked to this extremist milieu is the 2015 Duma arson attack, in which a Palestinian family’s home was firebombed, killing three members of the Dawabshe family and drawing widespread condemnation (Al Jazeera, 2018). Reports also document frequent confrontations between Hilltop Youth activists and Israeli security forces, as the group’s anti-state ideology increasingly targets both Palestinians and the Israeli military (Garcia-Navarro, 2012; Holcman, 2025).

Recent years have seen a marked escalation in settler violence, with over 1,000 attacks documented in the West Bank following the October 7, 2023 Hamas attacks (Perliger, 2025). Analysts note that while the Hilltop Youth lacks formal hierarchy, certain figures such as Meir Ettinger, the grandson of Rabbi Kahane, have been identified by Israeli authorities as ideological leaders or influential spokespeople (Magid, 2021). The movement’s internal culture is shaped by separatism and resistance to modernity; members often avoid digital technology and mainstream communication platforms as part of a broader effort to maintain ideological purity and communal isolation (Rosenberg & Vogelman-Natan, 2023).

International governments have recently taken formal action in response to rising extremist settler violence. In 2024, both the United States and the European Union designated entities and individuals tied to the Hilltop Youth as extremist actors. The U.S. Department of the Treasury issued sanctions citing arson, assaults, and targeted attacks on Palestinian civilians (U.S. Department of the Treasury, 2024), while the EU imposed asset freezes and visa bans on affiliated individuals and organizations for their involvement in serious human rights abuses in the West Bank (Council of the European Union, 2024). These measures reflect growing global recognition of the group’s role in destabilizing the region and contributing to the rise of far-right religious extremism in Israel.
