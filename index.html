What the Project Is

Topic, research focus, question, and creative website.

Topic Research Focus Question Creative Project
Research Topic

The Hilltop Youth, a radical Jewish nationalist youth movement based in informal outposts across the West Bank.

Research Focus
  • Origins of the movement
  • Core ideology and theological influences
  • On-the-ground activities and tactics
  • Political and religious structures that sustain them
Guiding Research Question
How does the Hilltop Youth movement represent contemporary religious extremism within Judaism, and what political, ideological, and social factors contribute to its persistence and influence in Israeli society?
Creative Project

An educational, research-based website that:

  • Explains the group’s history, worldview, and actions
  • Organizes academic findings into accessible sections
  • Uses visuals and layout to support learning and discussion

What I Learned

Key insights about origins, ideology, activities, and impact.

Origins Ideology Activities Significance
Origins

The Hilltop Youth emerged after the Gaza disengagement and intensifying settlement tensions, as young activists who increasingly rejected state authority, formal institutions, and mainstream settler leadership.

Ideology
  • Blend of religious Zionism and neo-Kahanist ideas
  • Strong emphasis on Jewish territorial maximalism
  • Anti-modern, separatist identity that distances itself from wider Israeli society
Activities
  • Price-tag attacks after demolitions or political decisions
  • Outpost building on hilltops without formal authorization
  • Confrontations with Palestinians and Israeli authorities
Contemporary Significance
International sanctions, growing media attention, and rising concern about settler extremism show that this youth movement has impacts far beyond its small numbers.

Why It Matters

The significance and importance of this research project.

Extremism Youth Global Patterns
Overlooked Extremism

The project brings attention to forms of Jewish extremism that are often overshadowed by other kinds of terrorism and radicalization in media and public debate.

Religion & Youth Rebellion
  • Shows how religious ideas can motivate direct action on the ground
  • Highlights the role of youth identity, autonomy, and defiance of authority
  • Illustrates how “fringe” groups can still influence broader conversations
From Local Case to Global Lens

The Hilltop Youth offer a focused case that helps us think about:

  • Vigilantism and private violence in contested spaces
  • How states respond to extremist actors from within their own majority
  • Patterns that echo in other religious and nationalist movements worldwide
Understanding this movement encourages a more nuanced, comparative view of extremism across different religions, regions, and political contexts.

Organizing the Research

The website translates academic research into a clear, guided experience, turning chapters and articles into:

  • Overview and context
  • Origins and historical background
  • Ideology and theology
  • Activities and documented incidents
  • Sources and further reading
Visual & Textual Design

Layout, imagery, and sectioning are chosen to:

  • Break down a sensitive topic into manageable pieces
  • Pair strong visuals with careful, sourced explanation
  • Encourage critical thinking instead of simple “good vs. bad” narratives
From Paper to Public Resource
The creative project is not just a summary of a research paper—it is a teaching tool designed to spark informed conversation among students, educators, and anyone trying to understand this movement more deeply.
